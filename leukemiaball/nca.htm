About NCA:

A 501(c)3 organization, The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society is the world's largest voluntary health organization dedicated to funding blood cancer research, education and patient services. Since its founding in 1949, the Society has invested more than $424 million for research specifically targeting blood cancers.

The National Capital Area Chapter of The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society is one of the top revenue-generating chapters for the Society, raising over $12 million dollars in FY06. The chapter serves the metropolitan Washington, DC area including Montgomery and Prince George counties in Maryland; Fairfax, Loudoun, Arlington, Alexandria, and Prince William counties in Virginia; and Washington, DC.

The chapter supports many patient services events, seminars and programs. E-mail Patient Services Manager, Sarah Singer, at sarah.singer@lls.org for more information.

Other Ways to Get Involved with the Society

Team In Training: TNT is the world's largest endurance sports training program. Train with TNT to run or walk a full or half marathon, cycle a century ride or complete a triathlon. For more information check out www.teamintraining.org/nca or contact Caitlin Brown at caitlin.brown@lls.org.

Light The Night: The Light The Night® Walk is The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society's nationwide evening fundraising Walk to celebrate and commemorate lives touched by cancer. In 2006, the National Capital Area Chapter anticipates 7,000 participants at five Walk locations throughout the Washington, DC metropolitan area. Please contact Teresa Traxler at teresa.traxler@lls.org or check out our website at www.lightthenight.org/nca for more information.

Man & Woman of the Year: Each year, hundreds of men and women in 41 cities throughout the nation vie for the title of Man & Woman of the Year. All candidates vying for the title of Man & Woman of the Year agree to utilize their leadership abilities to conduct their own 10-week fundraising campaigns for the Society. For more information, please contact Katie Goode at katie.goode@lls.org.

Pennies for Patients: Students across the country are collecting pennies, nickels, dimes and quarters during Pennies for Patients campaigns benefiting the Society. Please contact Erin Walker at erin.walker@lls.org for more information.

Major Giving: There are two ways to become a major donor with The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society. The first is by becoming a member of The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society's de Villiers Society through a significant outright gift. The second is by including the Society as part of your estate plan. For more than 50 years, The Leukemia & Lymphoma Society has sponsored research that has been a driving force behind breakthroughs in treating blood-related cancers. Because of your crucial help, new discoveries leading to improved treatment and cures for leukemia, lymphoma, Hodgkin's disease and myeloma are on the horizon.

For more information, please contact Lorraine Kammerer at lorraine.kammerer@lls.org.
