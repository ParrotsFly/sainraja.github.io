92 students to compete in spelling bee Monday
March 7, 2007

Ninety-two students from Loudoun County Public Schools, county private schools and home-schoolers will participate Monday, March 19 in the 25th annual Loudoun County Spelling Bee. The event begins at 7 p.m. in the auditorium of Stone Bridge High School at Claiborne Parkway and Hay Road.

The winner will represent the county in the Scripps Howard National Spelling Bee, May 30-31, in Washington.

Judges for Monday’s competition will be Sherri Simmons, a dean at Seneca Ridge Middle School; Elizabeth Dotur, a retired principal who coordinated the first six Loudoun County spelling bees; Elizabeth Greenblatt, a secretary at Rolling Ridge Elementary; and Lorin Buck, copy editor of the Loudoun Times-Mirror.
