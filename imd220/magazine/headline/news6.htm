No new housing for one year?
March 7, 2007

Next month, Loudoun County leaders will be considering an initiative to stall any new residential rezoning applications in Loudoun until January 2008. If approved, the plan would also put a stop to all proposed changes in 2007 to the county’s comprehensive plan that would have increased housing density.

Supervisor Jim Burton (I-Blue Ridge) said it was an attempt to encourage state leaders to address the worsening problem of snarled roads in the county. He said his proposal was inspired by similar actions in Prince William County.
